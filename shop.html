Green Tea Polyphenols

Green tea polyphenols are natural bioactive compounds extracted from Camellia sinensis leaves. Rich in catechins like EGCG, they offer strong antioxidant, antimicrobial, and anti-inflammatory benefits. This water-soluble, fine powder is ideal as a natural feed additive to enhance animal immunity, reduce oxidative stress, and improve gut health. It supports antibiotic-free livestock production and is suitable for pigs, poultry, cattle, and aquaculture.

  • Product name: Tea Polyphenols
  • Scientific name: Camellia Sinensis O. Ktze
  • Season: Spring and summer
  • Part: Leaf
  • Extraction Type: Solvent Extraction
  • Specification: 50% - 98%
  • Form: Powder
  • Test: HPLC
  • Shelf Life: 2 Years

Green Tea Extract Production Process

Step 1: Raw Material Cleaning

Green tea leaves are carefully cleaned to remove dust and impurities.

Step 2: Extraction with Ethanol

Leaves are placed into the extraction tank; ethanol is added gradually at 2–3 times the volume.

Step 3: Heating

The extraction tank is gently heated until the mixture reaches desired temperature.

Step 4: Vacuum Concentration

The extract is concentrated under low-temperature vacuum to retain bioactives.

Step 5: Condensation & Solvent Recovery

Steam is condensed and solvent is recycled into the system, ensuring sustainability.

Step 6: Spray Drying

The concentrated extract is spray-dried to produce fine polyphenol powder.
